Soybean Farm

Introduction/Background

Literature Review

"Our topic of choice is Advanced Soybean Agriculture, more specifically predicting soybean yield based on salicylic acid treatment, genotype, and water stress. Pre–existing research has found that salicylic acid treatments of 100-200 parts per million result in significantly higher soybean yields [1]. Furthermore, genotypes with larger roots systems tend to have water retention rates, which also results in higher yields [2]. Higher levels of water stress are often associated with lower plant height and weight, leaf size, and overall seed yield [3].

Dataset Description

Our dataset is “Advanced Soybean Agricultural Dataset” from Kaggle. This dataset consists of parameters including genotype, salicylic acid treatment, and water stress. There are 6 different genotypes, 3 levels of salicylic acid treatment: 250 mg, 450 mg, and 0 mg, and 2 levels of water stress: 5% and 70%. Furthermore, the dataset shows various quantitative metrics based on parameters such as plant height, biological weight, number of pods, and many others.

Problem Definition

Problem

Using current agricultural practices, it is difficult to predict and optimize soybean yield and health. This leads to inconsistencies that can potentially cause supply chain disruptions, price instability, inefficient allocation of resources, and an overall decline in quality and output. With the soybean industry already fraught with supply chain bottlenecks, it is imperative to determine the most efficient method to yield the highest quantity and quality of soybeans in order to meet rising global demand.

Methods

TTo preprocess the data, normalization with sklearn.ensemble library’s preprocess.normalize() function will scale features to a common range, preventing certain features from overly dominating in weights and loss functions, and making outlier detection easier [4]. The next step involves using scipy.stats library’s zscore() function to remove outliers preventing their distorting impacts on loss functions [5]. Finally, unique feature reduction algorithms will be employed for all three supervised ML algorithms to reduce dimensionality and computational complexity [6]. For Random Forest, sklearn.ensemble library’s RandomForestRegressor() function will be utilized to identify features for future removal, leveraging Random Forest’s strong tolerance for outliers and inherent feature reduction capabilities [7], [8]. This allows for immediate implementation of Random Forest after normalization, creating baseline predictions and giving a feature importance ranking for the midterm report [9]. Subsequently, a Neural Network can be implemented to identify complex interactions between variables and further improve model accuracy, with sklearn.inspection library’s permutation_importance() function preventing overfitting, improving the neural networks accuracy [10]. Lastly, a Bayesian Neural Network (BNN) will be created to complement the high accuracy of the Neural Network and Random Forest while addressing areas of high uncertainty [11]. Sklearn.decomposition library’s PCA() function will be used for the BNN’s dimensionality reduction, enhancing speed while retaining variance and preserving the underlying uncertainty in the model’s predictions [12].
